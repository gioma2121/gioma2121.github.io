03
Research/NLP

Subgroup Discovery with LLMs

Sentiment models work fine on average. The interesting question is where they systematically fail. I built a framework that uses LLMs to auto-generate features from unstructured text, then runs subgroup discovery to surface the cohorts where a classifier underperforms, teaching models to audit themselves. The LLM-generated features catch patterns hand-crafted ones miss.

ML Engineering/Interpretability

Fine-tuning CNNs, ViTs & LLMs for Medical AI

Fine-tuned ResNet-18 and ViT-B/16 on DermaMNIST, then interpreted predictions via GradCAM and Attention Rollout, exposing clinically relevant failure modes on the Melanoma/Nevi pair where the model was confidently wrong. 05
BSc Thesis · 109/110/Time series

Macrofinancial Effects of US Political Cycles

Can deep learning surface how US political cycles shape macrofinancial dynamics? I built and compared Temporal Fusion Transformer and LSTM models on a 40-year daily dataset of economic, financial, and sentiment indicators. TFT's attention is genuinely suited to multi-horizon forecasting, then I interpreted it via native attention weights and LIME to surface regime-dependent patterns. That interpretability layer is what made the thesis worth reading.
